About the model score

For each parameter, the number of cells representing presence within the study area are summed and then multiplied by the weight applied before running the model. This value is then divided by the total number of cells in the study area. All individual parameter scores are then summed to produce the total study area score. The score does not represent a standard metric, but is valuable when comparing between model results for different study areas or weighting schemes.
